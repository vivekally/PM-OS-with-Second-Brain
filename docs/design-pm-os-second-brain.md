## Problem Statement

The real goal is to validate the **second-brain concept** and ship it for the
persona the founder ultimately cares about: **lawyers**. PM is chosen as the
*first* persona because the founder knows it well and can build and learn fast.
PM OS is therefore both a product and a research vehicle.

The underlying problem for PMs: they can't use AI for the high-judgment core of
their job (strategy, discovery, prioritization) because general AI tools have no
persistent, structured context about their product, users, metrics, and
decisions. Re-explaining that context every session costs more than the output
saves, so AI stays stuck on low-stakes chores. PMBrain is a persistent,
structured, eventually self-evolving model of the PM's product world that makes
AI outputs good enough for real work.

## Demand Evidence

Current tier: **founder conviction (Tier C)**. No paying user, no named current
user, no behavioral demand yet. Soft FOMO signal from a product-community
meetup. Category demand proven by creator/audience-led products (Aakash Gupta PM
OS, growthpioneer PM OS, Paweł Huryn PM Brain / `phuryn/pm-skills`, mysecond.ai),
whose traction comes from distribution, not product superiority. None of this is
direct demand for this product.

## Status Quo

PMs use ad-hoc ChatGPT/Claude with no memory (re-explain context every session)
AND barely use AI for real PM work. Cost of the status quo is still
**unquantified** (see The Assignment).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** the founder's past self — a PM responsible for strategy/vision
and execution. **No current named user** (the key gap; the whole phased plan
depends on real PM adoption the founder does not yet have).

**Wedge (Phase 1, kept as conceived):** a guided, **couch-to-5k-style context
builder**. Instead of a blank canvas, it walks a PM through capturing their
product context in progressive sessions (product + ICP, then metrics + goals,
then bets + roadmap, then key decisions). Output is a **portable context
directory** the PM drops into Claude and ChatGPT and uses immediately. This is
the atomic unit of the second-brain concept, ships in days, gives value with
zero lock-in, and the guided format solves the cold-start problem every second
brain has (an empty brain is worthless).

Inspiration: couchto5k.ai (guided progressive program), mysecond.ai (second
brain). Differentiation vs. a bare Claude Project is the *speed and structure*
of guided capture — that delta is the wedge and must be proven.

## The Founder's Plan (4 phases)

1. **Phase 1 — Build PM context.** Fast guided UI to build context; users use it
   as a directory with Claude and ChatGPT. (couchto5k.ai, mysecond.ai)
2. **Phase 2 — Skill orchestration.** Replicate skill orchestration from the
   PM-OS products and mysecond.ai; target a GStack-like orchestration layer.
3. **Phase 3 — Two interaction modes.** Claude Code / cowork AND a UI-based app.
4. **Phase 4 — Build PMBrain.** A self-evolving brain inspired by GBrain, built
   on everything learned from real PM usage in phases 1–3.

## Premises

- **P1 (agreed, CEO-revised):** Core problem is *missing context*. But the brain
  *mechanism* is commoditizing (Perplexity Brain, etc. — see Strategic
  Positioning). The moat is the vertical structure + workflows on a controlled
  (GBrain-class, self-hostable) substrate, NOT the self-evolving mechanism.
- **P2 (agreed):** The wedge must build a context habit. Guided progressive
  capture (couch-to-5k) is how the brain gets seeded and fed.
- **P3 (agreed):** Distribution decides winners; the founder has no audience yet,
  so a GTM motion must run in parallel from day one.
- **P4 (revised this session):** The PM→founder→lawyer replicability is no longer
  treated purely as a distraction. PM is an explicit *research vehicle* for the
  lawyer second brain. BUT the transferable layer is **unnamed** (see Open
  Questions) — until named, this is a PM product with a lawyer dream attached.
- **NP — manual before auto (agreed, founder-led):** You cannot auto-evolve a
  brain well until you've watched what PMs maintain by hand. Phase 1's manual
  usage is the training signal for Phase 4. Manual-before-auto is correct, so the
  brain is built *after* real usage, not first.

## Strategic Positioning (CEO review — 2026-06-22, HOLD SCOPE + reframe)

### Market reality (June 2026)
- The self-evolving-brain *primitive* is commoditizing at the platform layer:
  Perplexity shipped "Brain" (self-improving memory, context graph, learns
  overnight) on 2026-06-18; Anthropic Cowork, OpenAI, Mem, Notion, Tana are in
  the same race. AI-memory market ~$6.27B (2026) heading to ~$28B (2030).
- The lawyer endgame is an $11B battlefield: Harvey ($11B valuation, $200M
  raised, 500+ legal agents, Legal Agent Benchmark) sells "institutional
  intelligence" (firm matter-context across multi-step workflows) via its
  DeepJudge partnership. 41 of the top-100 US firms have integrated GenAI.

### The reframe (revised premise)
The brain *mechanism* is plumbing, not the moat. The moat is the **vertical
domain structure** (a structured model of a PM's product / ICP / metrics / bets /
decisions) plus the **workflows** that consume it. Adopt a GBrain-class,
self-hostable memory substrate; do NOT invent a self-evolving brain. Own the
structure and workflows; operate the mechanism rather than rebuild it. Closed
platform memory is rejected as the substrate because it cannot hold a custom
PM/legal schema and cannot deploy into a confidential legal environment.

### Foundation decision (user, D2)
**Phases 1-3: optimize for PM speed (Option B).** Build the fastest PM product;
accept a possible foundation revisit at the legal pivot.
**Option A is documented as an optional route in the PRD for the tech team to
decide.** Option A = five low-cost choices that keep the legal door open without
building legal features now:
1. Own the data store (GBrain-class, self-hostable) — already chosen.
2. Tenant/owner/scope field on every context object from the first schema.
3. Route LLM/embedding calls through your own layer (swappable to zero-retention
   / in-tenant inference later).
4. Encryption at rest + secrets hygiene.
5. Audit log of context reads/writes (also powers PM trust-instrumentation).
Deferred regardless of route: on-prem deployment, BYOK/CMK, SOC 2, ethical
walls, legal hold.

**CEO note for the tech team:** of the five, #1 (already chosen) and #2
(per-object scope field) are cheap insurance even under the speed-optimized B
path — retrofitting multi-tenancy / access-scoping is the classic painful
rewrite even *within* the PM product (multi-PM, teams). Treat #1-#2 as
do-now-anyway; #3-#5 as the deferrable legal-pivot bundle.

### Outside-voice resolutions (D4)
- **Demand (cross-model consensus):** untested demand is the core risk; the plan
  defers finding out. Mitigation: the falsification test in The Assignment, with a
  hard kill criterion and a gate on Phases 2-4.
- **Substrate vs speed contradiction:** resolved by reading "GBrain-class" as
  *adopt the already-installed gbrain*, not build memory infra. Adopt-not-build is
  low-cost and reconciles D1 (portability) with D2 (PM speed). The falsification
  test needs no substrate. Final substrate call belongs in /plan-eng-review.
- **Scope:** founder chose to build Phase 1 in parallel with the test (not a hard
  stop). Phases 2-4 remain gated on the test result.

## Approaches Considered (sequencing)

### Approach A: Brain-loop early (office-hours recommendation)
Ship P1, get usage, watch, then a thin auto-evolution spike, then infra. Tests
the research question early. Effort: weeks per stage.

### Approach B: Founder's original order
P1 → P2 (GStack-style orchestration) → P3 (two interfaces) → P4 (brain). Learn
the PM role deeply from real product usage, then build the brain on that
foundation. Risk: months of orchestration + dual-interface infra before the
core hypothesis (self-evolving brain) is tested.

### Approach C: Pure research spike first
Build only the smallest self-evolving loop; test trust before product. Most
honest to the research goal, least like a launchable product.

## Recommended Approach (reconciled)

The founder defended building Phases 1–3 before the brain, with sound reasoning:
each phase generates a *different* kind of learning the self-evolving brain needs.
Phase 1 teaches what context PMs maintain. Phase 2 (skill orchestration) teaches
which PM workflows they actually invoke and what context those skills draw on —
the brain feeds the skills, so this reveals what the brain must serve. Phase 3
teaches where they prefer to work. You cannot know what the brain should evolve
until you've watched all three in real use. This logic holds and is accepted:
**manual and observed before auto.**

The one guardrail that survives: **thin-to-learn, heavy-to-scale.** Build the
thinnest version of each phase that produces its learning, in order, and do not
build the heavy/scale version of any phase until usage proves it earns the
engineering:

> **Week 0:** watch one real PM (the assignment).
> **Phase 1:** guided manual brain, portable to Claude/ChatGPT. Ship in days.
> **Phase 2 (thin):** a few hand-wired skills that consume the context — enough
>   to learn which skills PMs reach for and what context each needs. Defer
>   GStack-grade orchestration until that usage justifies the build.
> **Phase 3 (thin):** start with ONE interface (Claude Code/cowork, where you
>   already live); add the UI when a user actually asks for it.
> **Phase 4:** build the self-evolving brain on everything observed — and pull a
>   single auto-evolution loop forward as a spike the moment Phase 1+2 usage tells
>   you what to evolve.

The risk this guardrail protects against: "skill orchestration like GStack" is a
multi-month system. Build the full framework before the wedge proves PMs maintain
context and reach for skills, and you've over-built on an untested hypothesis.
Thin versions give the same learning at a fraction of the cost.

Run a distribution motion in parallel from week one: a free, shareable artifact
that doubles as top-of-funnel and as brain-seeding intake.

## Open Questions

1. **Transferability core (top priority).** Name the layer that carries from a PM
   brain to a lawyer brain (capture UX? evolution loop? trust model?). Until
   named, the research framing is unproven. The *schema* almost certainly does
   not transfer (PM context is loose/fast/recoverable; legal context is
   citation-bound, confidential, auditable, malpractice-stakes).
2. **No current named PM.** The entire learn-from-usage plan is gated on adoption
   the founder does not have. Blocks everything.
3. **Cost of the status quo is unquantified.** Migraine or hangnail?
4. **Differentiation vs. Claude Projects / ChatGPT memory.** Guided capture must
   be dramatically faster and more structured than a blank Claude Project.
5. **Willingness to pay:** recurring SaaS vs. a $29 one-time template. Price,
   trigger, recurring value.
6. **Scope risk (thin-to-learn):** A thin Phase 2 (a few hand-wired skills) is on
   the learning path and worth building early. GStack-grade orchestration is a
   multi-month system — defer it until real skill usage justifies the framework.

## Success Criteria

- **Week 1:** 1 real *current* PM (not the founder) watched using a prototype on
  a real task, unprompted, said they'd use it again next week.
- **Weeks 4–6:** 3 PMs using the guided context builder weekly; ≥1 paying or
  formally committed. Observable: their 4th context-assisted output is better
  than their 1st because the brain knows more.
- **Brain spike:** one automated evolution loop produces an update a PM *trusts*
  and keeps, rather than corrects or ignores. This is the real research result.
- **Distribution:** a free artifact shared publicly brings signups with zero paid
  acquisition.

## Distribution Plan

- **Delivery:** web app (Phase 1+), plus a Claude Code/cowork interface — existing
  deploy pipeline covers the web app.
- **GTM (pick ONE primary to start):** free viral context tool whose output is
  shareable and seeds the brain; OR open-source a PM-skills layer for credibility;
  OR content/community to build the audience the founder lacks. Do not run all
  three at once.

## Dependencies

- **Access to 2–3 current PMs** to interview/watch — biggest blocker; none named.
- Brain architecture decision (reuse a GBrain-style self-evolving model).
- The single Phase-1 guided-capture flow and its portable output format.

## The Assignment (updated after outside-voice review)

Decision (D4): build Phase 1 AND run the falsification test in parallel. To keep
"parallel" from becoming "never run the test," the test is timeboxed and gates
Phases 2-4.

**Validation (this week — gates everything past Phase 1):**
1. Get ONE current PM (not your past self) on a 30-min call. Watch them use AI on
   a real strategy/spec task. Don't demo, don't help. Record the costliest pain
   moment and what it cost (minutes, rework, a second-guessed decision).
2. **Falsification test (the wedge kill-shot):** take one real PM's product
   context. Produce it two ways — (a) ~30-min hand-written plain Claude Project,
   (b) the structured "guided" version (the Phase-1 build can generate this). Run
   the same 3 real PM tasks (strategy memo, spec, prioritization) through both.
   - **Kill criterion:** if a neutral PM can't tell which output used structured
     context, or hand-paste reaches ~80% of the value, the wedge does not exist —
     stop and rethink before building Phases 2-4.
   - **Win payoff:** if structured wins clearly, that before/after comparison is
     your differentiation proof, your shareable distribution artifact, and your
     first-PM hook, all in one.

**Gate:** Phases 2-4 (skill orchestration, second interface, self-evolving brain)
do not start until the falsification test returns a clear "structured wins."
Phase 1 build may proceed in parallel because it produces the test's structured
artifact.
